Biological background
The paper focuses on myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome (ME/CFS), a chronic and poorly understood multisystem disease marked by disabling fatigue, immune disturbance, and post-exertional malaise (PEM). PEM is a worsening of symptoms after physical or mental exertion and is one of the defining clinical features of ME/CFS. Many patients report illness onset after an infection, and ME/CFS shares some symptomatic overlap with long COVID and other post-viral syndromes.


Paper’s dataset description
The data is single cell RNA dataset stored in an AnnData object made up of 473778 cells, collected from 30 ME/CFS patients and 28 sedentary healthy controls, with samples collected at baseline and 24 hours after CPET. Participants were matched for sex and BMI, and the samples were collected before the COVID-19 pandemic, reducing the possibility that the ME/CFS cohort included long COVID cases. The experimental design generated paired baseline and post-exercise immune-cell profiles, enabling both case-control comparisons and within-individual symptom-provocation analyses. Each sample contains detail metadata such as organism, sex, disease_state (case vs control), collection_day (documents pre and post exersise samples, D1 and D2), chromium_batch, individual_id (links samples between collection_day). Additionaly contains standard scRNA data, including gene counts, and quality metrics like mitochondrial and rRNA read percentages. The data has been QC filtered in gene and cell counts, normalised and log transformed.

    Study-design metadata:
    - disease_state: case, control
    - collection_day: D1, D2
    - PatientID: participant identifier linking paired D1 and D2 samples
    - SampleID: sample identifier
    - sex: female, male
    - chromium_batch: sequencing batch identifier

    Expression matrices:
    - adata.X: log1p-normalized expression (QC filtered)
    - adata.layers["counts"]: raw filtered counts
    - adata.layers["norm_1e4"]: normalized counts
    - adata.layers["log1p_norm"]: log1p-normalized counts
    - adata.var["highly_variable"]: highly variable gene flag
